Cloud Detection and Classification in Radiance Sky Images for Solar Energy Optimization Using Attention-SolarNet

Project Overview

This project presents Attention-SolarNet, a deep learning model for detecting and classifying clouds from sky images to enhance solar energy prediction. The architecture incorporates Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) with a spatial attention mechanism to dynamically emphasize cloud-relevant regions in images. The system improves solar irradiance forecasting by accurately identifying cloud types and minimizing prediction error. Jack used the NWPU-RESISC45 dataset, consisting of 31,500 images across 45 scene classes. Extensive preprocessing, balancing, and augmentation steps ensured robustness. Attention-SolarNet outperformed baseline CNNs with 95.76% test accuracy, 100% recall, and 94.00% F1-score, significantly reducing test loss to 14.89%.

Key Contributions

  • Developed and compared SolarNet and Attention-SolarNet architectures.
  • Integrated spatial attention mechanism to enhance cloud feature localization.
  • Achieved 95.76% test accuracy, 100% recall, and 94.00% F1-score with Attention-SolarNet.
  • Utilized Grad-CAM for visualization to enhance interpretability.
  • Deployed a GUI for real-time cloud classification and result analysis.

Conclusion

The Attention-SolarNet model significantly outperformed the baseline SolarNet in accurately detecting clouds under diverse conditions. Its attention mechanism provided improved focus on cloud regions, enabling better generalization and forecasting reliability for solar energy systems. The use of spatial attention was key in enhancing classification accuracy and robustness against overfitting. Grad-CAM visualization and GUI integration further improved interpretability and usability in real-world deployments.

Limitation

  • The attention mechanism increased training complexity and resource usage.
  • Slight trade-offs in precision due to more false positives in favor of perfect recall.
  • Performance is sensitive to dataset quality and may generalize poorly on low-resolution imagery.

Future Work

  • Optimize attention mechanisms for better efficiency and scalability.
  • Explore hybrid architectures combining attention with transfer learning and domain adaptation.
  • Expand datasets across seasons, geographies, and resolutions to improve generalization.
  • Integrate deployment tools for cloud-based or edge-device applications in solar energy management.
