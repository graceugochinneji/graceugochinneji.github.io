Project Overview

This project introduces Attention-PowerWiNet, a hybrid deep learning model combining PowerNet, WiNet, and an attention mechanism. It predicts wind power generation using two datasets—one seasonal and one continuous. The model captures both spatial and temporal dependencies in wind energy data and leverages SHAP for interpretability.

Key Contributions

  • Developed PowerNet, WiNet, PowerWiNet, and Attention-PowerWiNet architectures.
  • Evaluated model performance on two wind farm datasets.
  • Achieved best performance with Attention-PowerWiNet (MAE: 0.035, R²: 0.906).
  • Implemented SHAP interpretability with 5 types of plots.
  • Designed a user-friendly GUI for model deployment and result visualization.

Conclusion

Attention-PowerWiNet demonstrated superior performance across seasonal and continuous datasets. Its integration of attention mechanisms improved both prediction accuracy and model interpretability. The GUI implementation also enhances usability for stakeholders, including wind farm operators and energy managers.

Limitation

  • Limited features: relies heavily on environmental variables without considering geographical or turbine-specific data like topography or fan height.
  • Unassessed abnormal scenarios: model was not tested with extreme weather conditions or system faults.

Future Work

  • Incorporate additional physical attributes such as turbine height, terrain, and maintenance logs.
  • Simulate abnormal conditions (noise, missing data, anomalies) to improve robustness and generalization.
  • Expand real-time deployment capabilities with backend integration and adaptive learning.
