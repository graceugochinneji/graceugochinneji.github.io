Stacked-Ensemble CNN for
Dog-Breed Classification

Project Overview

Josephine Qiao designed an ensemble model that stacks three transfer-learned CNNs—VGG16, ResNet-50 and Inception V3—to recognise 120 dog breeds. The stacking strategy, coupled with fine-tuning and targeted data-augmentation, delivers high accuracy while mitigating the over-fitting observed in single-network baselines.:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Key Contributions

  • Implemented three transfer-learned models and merged them with a stacking meta-learner.
  • Applied rotation, translation, zoom, shear and brightness jitter to balance minority breeds.
  • Introduced early-stopping and learning-rate tuning to curb over-fitting and speed convergence.
  • Achieved 90 % test accuracy  (Macro-F1 ≈ 0.89) — a 3-12 pp boost over the best single network.:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • Packaged the final model in a Flask web app for drag-and-drop breed prediction.

Results Snapshot

  • Accuracy : 90.0 %
  • Macro Precision / Recall / F1 : 0.89 / 0.89 / 0.89
  • AUC : 0.97  Top-5 Accuracy : 98 %
  • Outperformed prior single-CNN dog-breed studies by >20 pp on the same dataset.:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Limitations

  • Model size (~83 M parameters) limits deployment on very low-power devices.
  • Trained on daylight images—performance under low-light or occlusions needs validation.
  • Relies on Kaggle dog-face crops; full-body or multi-dog scenes may confuse predictions.

Future Work

  • Distil the ensemble into a lightweight mobile model without significant accuracy loss.
  • Add attention for body-pose cues and expand to mixed-breed recognition.
  • Collect in-the-wild photographs to improve robustness and test cross-domain generalisation.
