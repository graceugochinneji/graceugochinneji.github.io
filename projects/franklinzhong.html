FranklinNet — Spatial-Attention CNN Ensemble for Image Classification

Project Overview

Franklin Zhong proposes FranklinNet, an ensemble that blends Inception-V2, Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) blocks, and ResNet backbones with a lightweight spatial-attention layer. The design tackles diverse image-classification tasks—human faces, diseased plant leaves, and skin-lesion photos—while maintaining high accuracy and robustness across lighting, quality, and class-imbalance challenges.:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Key Contributions

  • Integrated InceptionV2 + SE + ResNet blocks under a unifying spatial-attention head to form FranklinNet.:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • Employed extensive data-augmentation and class-balancing to mitigate long-tail effects.
  • Validated on three benchmark datasets with state-of-the-art or near-SOTA results.
  • Provided Grad-CAM heat-maps for interpretability and a Tkinter GUI for end-users.:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Results Snapshot

  • Human Face : Accuracy = 0.98, Loss = 0.05, AUC = 0.99
  • Plant Disease : Accuracy = 0.96, Loss = 0.19, AUC = 0.99
  • Skin Cancer : Accuracy = 0.90, Loss = 0.26, AUC = 0.96

These metrics confirm FranklinNet’s versatility across visually and contextually distinct domains.:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Limitations

  • Model size and compute demand limit deployment on low-power devices.:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Domain adaptation remains untested for unseen data sources (e.g., different cameras, clinics).
  • Imbalanced classes in real-world scenarios may still degrade performance despite augmentation.

Future Work

  • Use GAN-based synthetic augmentation to enrich rare categories.:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Apply pruning and quantization for mobile/edge deployment.
  • Explore multi-task learning to bolster generalisation across domains.
